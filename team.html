Co-Director

Sharron McPherson Foxx Powerful Long Ladder Executive Administrative and Production Assistant has worked in many capacities either as Director, dancer, singer, choreographer at Karamu Theater, Chagrin Valley Little Theater, Near West Theater, Dobama, Lakeland Community College, East Cleveland Theater. Career highlights includes, playing the iconic role of "Benetha Younger" in "A Raisin in the Sun", directing "The Lynching of Effie Childs" by Gwen Wright, receiving a Karamu Acting Award winner for the production of "Constant Star", directing August Wilson's, "Joe Turners Come and Gone", working as lighting and sound operator for Powerful Long Ladder's award winning production "Lady Day at Emerson Bar and Grille" and founding a women's theater group, Maiden Voyage Theatre Ensemble in 2013, which specializes in the production of theatre by and for women. She is currently Artist is Resident with CSU Black Studies Department, working along with Prester Picket in the Howard A. Mims, Cultural Center. Sharron has worked with and taken workshops with some notable names in theatre, such as Margaret Ford Taylor, Dance Workshops with Chuck Davis, Urban Bush Women, Sabitino and Barbara Verlezzo and Bill T. Jones. She was a dancer with DuffyLit Ensemble here in Cleveland and very honored to be mentored by Dance Legend Marjorie Witt Johnson. Sharron works as assistant vocal coach and costumer with Cleveland's "Showagon" performing arts troupe. She's a singer with Afterthought and the NuSoul band, as well as "Wish You Were Here" the acclaimed Pink Floyd Tribute Band, and has been an Arts Educator for over 10 years. Sharron is proud to work with Terrence Spivey and share the exciting, innovative mission and creative vision of Powerful Long Ladder Ensemble.
